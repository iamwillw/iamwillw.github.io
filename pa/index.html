Protest Access: Social Justice for All

Protest Access is a volunteer collective focused on Black Lives Matter and anti-racism content on social media. We provide captions, transcripts and visual descriptions as requested by online communities:

  • d/Deaf
  • Hard of Hearing (HOH)
  • Blind
  • Visually Impaired (VI)
  • International and American audiences where English is a second language

Justice and accessibility are inextricably linked. We're committed to making the largest social and cultural movement of our lifetime accessible. Our volunteers are working to make it easier for everyone to engage in these discussions.

Volunteer

We're always in need of more volunteers in our content execution roles:

  • transcribing
  • visual support
  • captioning
  • video preparation

We process new applications daily to make on-boarding as quick and smooth as possible. We also add motivated and involved volunteers to our organizing team as the need arises.

How to get involved

To volunteer with us, fill out an application. If you have any questions about volunteering, send us an email.

Fundraising

Protest Access, started from a small group, now has over 200 volunteers from all over the world. They caption, transcribe and visually describe content. About 30 of those volunteers focus on keeping operations functioning. Volunteers are:

  • young and old,
  • disabled and abled,
  • Black and non-Black.

As we have grown, so has our need for tools to handle our ever-expanding workload.

How you can help

To continue to provide these essential services, we have set a goal of raising $5,000. All funds raised would:

  • go towards the functioning of our volunteer collective, and
  • ensure greater internal sustainability and efficiency.

Covering expenses enables us to focus more time on processing our ever-growing request list.

Follow us on social media to be the first to know when we launch our GoFundMe campaign.
